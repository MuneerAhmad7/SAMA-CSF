# Cybersecurity Governance Charter
**Maps to:** SAMA CSF 1.1/1.2, NCA ECC 1‑1, 1‑2

## 1. Purpose
Establishes the organizational structure, authority, and operating model for cybersecurity governance.

## 2. Organizational Structure
```
                    Board of Directors
                          │
        Board Risk & Compliance Committee
                          │
              Cyber Security Committee  ← cross-functional (IT, Legal, HR, Ops, Business Units)
                          │
                        CISO  ← independent from IT, reports to [CEO/Board]
                     /    |    \
              Security  GRC   SOC/IR
               Eng.    Team    Team
```

## 3. CISO Mandate
- Independent from IT operations (segregation of duties — SAMA CSF 1.1, ECC 1‑2‑6)
- Authority to mandate security requirements across all business units
- Direct escalation path to [CEO / Board Committee] without IT filtering
- Annual performance objectives tied to cybersecurity KPIs/KRIs

## 4. Cyber Security Committee
| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Chair | CISO or Board-appointed Chair |
| Members | CISO, CIO, Head of Risk, Head of Legal/DPO, Head of HR, Business Unit reps |
| Frequency | Quarterly minimum (monthly recommended in first 12 months) |
| Mandate | Approve risk treatment decisions, review KPIs/KRIs, escalate to Board |
| Secretariat | GRC team maintains minutes, action log, decision register |

## 5. Cybersecurity Strategy
- Horizon: 3–5 years, reviewed annually
- Inputs: threat landscape analysis, business strategy, regulatory roadmap (SAMA/NCA/SDAIA), prior year KPI/KRI performance
- Approval: Board Risk & Compliance Committee

## 6. Escalation Matrix
| Severity | Escalate to | Timeframe |
|---|---|---|
| Critical incident | CISO → CEO → Board (+ SAMA/NCA notification) | Immediate / within 2h |
| High risk unresolved > SLA | Cyber Security Committee | Next meeting |
| Policy exception | CISO | Prior to implementation |

## 7. Review
This charter is reviewed annually alongside the Cybersecurity Policy.
