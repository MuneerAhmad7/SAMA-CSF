# Security Awareness & Training Policy
**Maps to:** SAMA CSF 1.6, 1.7, NCA ECC 1‑10

## 1. Purpose
Ensure every employee — and especially those with privileged or sensitive access — can recognize and resist social engineering attempts across every channel an attacker might use, not just email.

## 2. Mandatory Training
| Course | Audience | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Annual Cybersecurity Awareness | All Staff | Annual |
| Phishing Recognition & Reporting | All Staff | Annual |
| Vishing & Social Engineering Awareness | All Staff | Annual |
| Secure Remote Work & Data Handling | All Staff | Annual |
| New Hire Security Induction | All Staff | Once, within first 2 weeks |
| Role-based courses (Secure Coding, Payment Fraud Awareness, Privileged Access) | As applicable | Annual |

## 3. Simulation Requirements
- Email phishing simulations: **quarterly minimum**.
- Vishing (phone-based social engineering) simulations: **at least twice yearly**, specifically targeting employees with privileged or sensitive tool access — this is the control gap identified in the 2020 Twitter breach.
- Any employee who fails a simulation is automatically assigned follow-up training and re-tested within 30-60 days.
- Any employee who fails **two or more** simulations within a rolling 12-month period is flagged for individual follow-up with their manager and the CISO.

## 4. Verification Procedure for Access/Credential Requests
Regardless of contact channel (phone, email, chat), no employee may action a credential reset, access grant, or account change based solely on a requester's self-identification. All such requests must be verified through a secondary, pre-established channel before action is taken. This procedure itself must be included in awareness training content, not just documented separately.

## 5. Privileged Access Considerations
Employees with access to sensitive internal tools or systems (support tools, admin panels, payment systems) receive additional, targeted awareness training and are prioritized for vishing simulation testing, given their elevated risk profile.

## 6. Measurement & Reporting
- Training completion tracked per employee, reported to the Cyber Security Committee quarterly.
- Simulation failure rates tracked by channel (email, vishing, smishing) and by department, to identify systemic gaps rather than only individual ones.

## 7. Review
Reviewed annually or immediately following any awareness-related security incident.
